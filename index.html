Arrows, and arrows, and arrows...

Uncle Qiang's house consists of a $2026 \times 2026$ grid of $1 \times 1$ rooms. Every room has four walls, exactly one of which contains an outgoing one way door. If the wall is between two adjacent rooms, then the door leads from one room to the other. If the wall is on the perimeter of the house then the door leads outside. In every room, each of the four walls has at most one door, which leads either into or out of the room. There are no incoming doors from outside of the house.

Uncle Qiang wants to redesign the house by performing moves: in a move, he chooses a room whose four walls are not all occupied by doors. He then removes that room's outgoing one-way door and replaces it with a new outgoing one-way door through one of its walls not occupied by a door.

Uncle Qiang would like to perform a series of up to $k$ moves so that it is possible to exit the house from any room by following only outgoing one-way doors. Find the smallest number $k$ such that this is always possible.

Combinatorics or Number Theory?

Brian and Stewie are decorating an infinite street of house numbered $1, 2, \dots$ where every house is painted either Red or Blue. Stewie is a bit particular about on how the house must be painted. Let $R(n)$ and $B(n)$ be the number of divisors of $n$ which are painted Blue respectively. Stewie insists that the following rules must be followed.

  • If $n$ is a perfect square, then $R(n) = B(n) + 1$.
  • If $n$ is not a perfect square, then $R(n) = B(n)$.
Show that if house $x$ and house $y$ are of the same color, then house $x \cdot y$ must be Red.

Sets and Powers of Two

Let $n$ be a positive integer. Initially the sequence $0,0,\cdots,0$ ($n$ times) is written on the board. In each round, Ananya choses an integer $t$ and a subset of the numbers written on the board and adds $t$ to all of them. What is the minimum number of rounds in which Ananya can make the sequence on the board strictly increasing?
